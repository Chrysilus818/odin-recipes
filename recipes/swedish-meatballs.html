Why try Swedish Meatballs?

Some of you may be wondering, why would I want to try Swedish Meatballs? Aren't they the same as regular meatballs? Oh, no, no, no, they are most definetely not! Think of Swedish Meatballs as smaller, better tasting Italian meatballs in a rich gravy sauce. They are so good, IKEA is rumored to sell over 1 BILLION meatballs a year. The best part of Swedish Meatballs is that they are easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • Ground Beef
  • Bread Crumbs
  • Parsley
  • Allspice and Nutmeg
  • Onion
  • Garlic Powder
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Egg
  • Olive Oil
  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Beef Broth
  • Heavy Cream
  • Worcestershire Sauce
  • Dijon Mustard

Cooking Instructions

These cook very quickly, so from assembly to prepared dinner should be about 30 minutes.

  1. In a medium sized bowl, mix the beef, bread crumbs, parsley, all spice, nutmeg, onion, garlic powder, salt, pepper, and egg.
  2. After mixing, roll the mixture into approximately 20 small meatballs. Also heat your skillet with olive oil and 1 tablespoon of butter.
  3. Once the skillet is hot, place the meatballs onto the skillet and cook them until brown and cooked throughout. Make sure you turn over the meatballs while they are cooking to brown them on all sides. After the meatballs are cooked, transfer them to a plate and cover.
  4. Using the same skillet you used to cook the meatballs in, add 4 tablespoons of butter and flour and whisk until the mixture browns. Slowly add the beef broth and heavy cream. Next, add the worcestershire sauce and mustard. Bring all the ingredients to a simmer until it starts to thicken.
  5. Lastly, add the meatballs back into the skillet and simmer for another couple minutes. Add salt and pepper to taste.
